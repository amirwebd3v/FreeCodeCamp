The Remarkable Life of Ada Lovelace

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace, was an English mathematician and writer who lived from 1815 to 1852. Here are some key highlights of her extraordinary life:

  • 1815 - Born to poet Lord Byron and Lady Byron
  • 1833 - Meets Charles Babbage and begins studying his Analytical Engine
  • 1842-43 - Translates Luigi Menabrea's article on the Analytical Engine
  • 1843 - Publishes her extensive notes, including the first algorithm intended for machine processing
  • 1852 - Passes away at the age of 36, leaving a legacy that would inspire generations of computer scientists

"The Analytical Engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform."

- Ada Lovelace
